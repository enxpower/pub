Brand Story

品牌故事

Heritage · Identity · Vision

华夏和 is born from the conviction that Chinese civilization carries a quiet luxury the modern world has yet to fully discover. Not the red lacquer of festivals, nor the gilded excess of export ceramics — but the scholar's stone, the monk's tea bowl, the breath between brushstrokes.

We exist to translate that depth into objects, rituals, and experiences that belong equally in Beijing and Paris, Tokyo and London.

华 Huá — Splendor

The character 华 speaks of florescence — of civilization in full bloom. It carries the weight of millennia: the arts, the sciences, the philosophy that the Chinese world refined across five thousand years. In our brand, 华 is the inheritance we draw from, and the standard we hold ourselves to.

夏 Xià — The Great Land

夏 names the ancient heartland — China's first dynasty, the origin myth of a civilization. It grounds 华夏和 in place and continuity, reminding us that the most enduring things grow slowly, from soil that has been worked for generations. Our craft is measured in seasons, not quarters.

和 Hé — Harmony

和 is the most essential character in the brand, and in Chinese philosophy itself. It is not mere peace — it is the dynamic balance between opposites. Hot and cold. Full and empty. Old and new. Every product we make, every space we inhabit, every line we draw aspires to this harmony.

Logo System

标志系统

Primary Mark · Variations · Usage Rules

The Huaxiahe logo is a monogram-mandala: the initials of 华夏和 (H, X, H) interlaced within a classical petal frame, with an incense burner (香炉) at its center. The form is simultaneously a seal, a crest, and a window lattice — referencing three pillars of Chinese material culture. The gradient transitions from warm ivory to deep bronze, reflecting the journey from raw material to refined craft.

Minimum Clear Space

The logo must always be surrounded by clear space equal to one-sixth of the logo's total height on all sides. This zone must remain free of all text, graphics, and page edges. Minimum reproduction size: 24px / 8mm. Below this threshold, use the icon mark only.

Incorrect Usage
✕ Do Not Rotate Never rotate or tilt the mark in any direction.
✕ Do Not Distort Never stretch or compress the proportions.
✕ Do Not Use Below Minimum Size Never reproduce below 24px. Use icon mark instead.
Color System

色彩体系

Primary Palette · Secondary Tones · Usage Rules

The palette is drawn from the logo's own gradient: a procession from kiln-white ivory through warm linen, natural gold, and settling into the deep amber of aged lacquerware. Colors are used with great restraint — the eye should always rest on negative space first.

Light Compositions

Use Warm White (#FAF8F5) or Linen (#F5F2EC) as canvas. Body text in Stone. Amber Deep for headings. Brand Gold for accents and rules. Generosity with negative space is the first rule of every layout.

Dark Compositions

Use Ink Black (#1A1612) as base. Ivory and Warm Linen for text hierarchy. Brand Gold for accent elements. Reserve full white for maximum contrast moments only. Avoid pure black backgrounds — always use the warm ink tone.

Gradient Use

The primary logo gradient (Ivory → Brand Gold → Amber) may be applied to typographic display moments, packaging ribbons, and brand seals. Never apply the gradient to body text or functional UI elements.

Color Restraint

No single layout should use more than three palette values simultaneously. Color should be earned — a single amber heading on a white page communicates more luxury than a page covered in gold. Silence is a color.

Typography System

字体系统

Type Scale · Roles · Pairing

The typographic identity pairs two serif traditions across two scripts. Cormorant Garamond brings classical European editorial grace — its narrow proportions and delicate hairlines mirror the refinement of Chinese fine-line engraving. Noto Serif SC grounds the Chinese type in the Song tradition: structured, unhurried, deeply readable at all scales.

Photography Direction

摄影方向

Light · Composition · Mood · Material

All Huaxiahe photography obeys a single principle: the image should feel like a pause. Natural side-light or diffuse north-facing window light only. No flash. No high-saturation edits. Backgrounds: white plaster, raw linen, aged wood, gray stone. Props are chosen for their texture, not their color.

◻

Product Shot

Hero Product

Single subject, generous negative space. 45° soft side-light. Warm white or stone gray background. No shadows cut hard. Shot from slightly above eye level to reveal vessel interiors.

◻

Lifestyle

Lifestyle

Hands in frame. The moment before or after, never during. Morning light through shoji screens. Tea being poured, not held. Incense smoke ascending, not lit.

◻

Macro Detail

Craft Detail

Ultra-close texture shots of grain, glaze, fiber, carving. Shallow depth of field. Shows the hours behind the object. This is where the brand premium is made visible.

◻

Ambient Scene

Space & Atmosphere

Wide scenes of ritual spaces — the tea table, the study, the window seat. Objects in use, not on display. The room breathes. No staged symmetry; asymmetric placement following classical Chinese still-life composition.

Packaging System

包装体系

Packaging is the first physical encounter with the brand. Every surface is treated with the same discipline as the product inside: uncoated natural stock, debossed or foil-stamped marks, minimal text. The unboxing should feel like opening a letter from a trusted friend.

Digital Presence

数字形象

Web · Mobile · Social · Email

Digital touchpoints follow the same restraint as print. Backgrounds stay warm, not stark white. Transitions are slow and intentional — no bounce, no spring. Typography is large; interface elements are minimal. Every screen should feel like turning the page of a book, not clicking through an app.

Brand Voice

品牌语调

Tone · Writing · Principles · Communication

Huaxiahe communicates the way a skilled host pours tea: with full attention, without hurry, without explanation. Words are chosen for what they carry, not what they show off. The register is intimate and literate — never academic, never commercial.

01
Unhurried

Never push, never announce. Let the reader arrive at the product as if by their own curiosity.

02
Concrete

Name materials, origins, and processes. "Sichuan cypress" not "natural wood." Precision earns trust.

03
Bilingual Parity

Chinese and English are equals. Neither is a translation of the other. Each speaks to its reader natively.

04
Silence Earns Space

Less copy is always more. One beautiful sentence outperforms five functional ones. Edit without mercy.

Writing Examples
